About

The global concern over climate change and rapid warming poses significant challenges for India, a country striving for sustainable development amidst economic ambitions. Existing climate models often have biases, particularly in predicting India's climate, resulting in high uncertainty and complicating policy and decision-making. Recognizing these pressing issues of climate change and the urgent need for actionable climate information for policymaking, IIT Delhi has established the DST Centre of Excellence in Climate Information, supported by the DST, GoI.

This initiative focuses on refining the recently developed India Centric Climate Modeling Framework (ICCMF) and integrating emerging techniques like machine learning and network science into climate science. The goal is to produce reliable climate projections tailored to India's specific needs. By delivering accurate regional climate simulations, this effort aims to support informed decision-making in agriculture, energy, health, and water resources, which are vital for India's economy and well-being in the face of global warming. Additionally, this initiative is committed to building capacity through formal training and academic engagements, ensuring a robust foundation for future climate resilience.

Join the DST Centre of Excellence in Climate Modeling at IIT Delhi.
